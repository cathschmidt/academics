hero image: an image of successful work or the moment that successful work is being installed. This image is responsible for setting the tone of the department and should reflect the energy of the department at RISD. Work that is "being installed" can be a final crit or the moment of displaying the work for the final crit, this allows a contrast between other majors in which the story is started with breathtaking work.

Architecture at RISD emphasizes personal artistic sensibilities and exploring the creative process as a means of defining and articulating your own approach to spatial design. As a student you will be empowered and equipped to succeed in the client-based practice of architecture while understanding your role as a cultural creator.

In the Studio

BArch Candidate: Tamara Tsond

“I came to RISD thinking that I’d like to study across a variety of disciplines, but I mostly just thought of it as a way to take a break from what I was doing architecturally. Instead, this way of thinking and working actually changed how I think of and approach my work. I quickly found that there’s this enormous hunger at RISD for interdisciplinary work that makes it easy for people to come together and collaborate. I had no idea how much that would influence my own way of thinking.”

senior Tamara Tsond»

Kyna Leski: Department Head

A principal at the busy architectural studio 3SIX0, Professor Kyna Leski finds that helping RISD students navigate through the creative process is both personally and professionally inspiring. In fact, in both her teaching and private practice, she focuses on the nature of creativity and how it translates across disciplines. »
