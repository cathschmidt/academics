In the Studio

BFA candidate: Margaret Kearney

"I really had no idea what it would mean to relate my environmental activism with my work in textiles, so it's been exciting to develop a process for myself." Margaret Kearney’s ability to weave formal and aesthetic concerns with her environmental activism won her national recognition as a Udall Scholar, a program that recognizes college students on the basis of their commitment to careers in the environment, health care or tribal public policy. In winning the scholarship – which is typically awarded to students majoring in science or environmental policy – Kearney became the first student from an art and design school to be recognized by the Udall Foundation since the awards were first given in 1996.

Student Work

Brooks Hagan: Department Head

Textile designer, artist and researcher Brooks Hagen inspires RISD students to continuously expand their knowledge and explore the use of advanced technologies in their work. Hagen maintains a studio practice centered on hand-production and digital design, and regularly collaborates with industry leaders. He recently completed a project with computer scientists at Cornell University, which investigates advanced computational environments for the design of constructed textiles.

“When I was looking at textile and apparel programs all over the country, someone said to me, ‘Well, if you want to be a cog in the wheel, then go to X school. But if you want to be the driver of the car, then you should go to RISD. And, I mean, who doesn’t want to be the driver of their own car, right? So, eventually I would like to do that — to drive my own design firm.”

Chriss Slevin BFA ‘97 PT / MFA ‘07 TX
