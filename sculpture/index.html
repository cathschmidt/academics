Even though we experience the world three-dimensionally, learning to think and make 3D objects is a fascinating challenge. In Sculpture at RISD, you'll learn to push beyond obvious solutions, to think independently and critically, to grow as an artist and to realize strong conceptual ideas through the informed use of materials.

In the Studio

Through intense hands-on work, you will gain the technical command needed to express your ideas with confidence. You’ll work with every material imaginable — including metal, wood, plastics, computer chips and more — to develop a true understanding of historical and contemporary sculptural issues.

Nafis White: Sophomore

“I came to RISD thinking that I’d like to study across a variety of disciplines, but I mostly just thought of it as a way to take a break from what I was doing architecturally. Instead, this way of thinking and working actually changed how I think of and approach my work. I quickly found that there’s this enormous hunger at RISD for interdisciplinary work that makes it easy for people to come together and collaborate. I had no idea how much that would influence my own way of thinking.”

Dean Snyder: Department Head

Dean Snyder was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He received a B.F.A. in photography and sculpture at the Kansas City Art Institute in 1974. In 1975-76 Snyder received a British Arts Council Fellowship for postgraduate work in sculpture at Lanchester Polytechnic, Coventry, UK. He completed his M.F.A. work in sculpture in 1978 at The School of The Art Institute of Chicago.

Sculpture alumni have a strong a command of the processes inherent to sculpture as a means of realizing ideas and expressing them in the world. They find success in nearly every field imaginable – as multimedia, special effect and installation artists, critics, art writers, curators, teachers and much more...
