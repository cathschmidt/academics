Top

Industrial Design (better known at RISD as ID) starts with exploring user experience and developing objects, products and systems that enable people to do things more easily and comfortably. It involves finding innovative ways to use creative thinking and the design process itself to solve problems and improve our everyday lives.

In the Studio

In responding to assigned projects, ID majors work with a wide range of materials in the process of developing ideas, carrying them through from sketches to developmental drawings, to 3D mock-ups and models, to working prototypes for objects, products, systems and experiences.

ID senior | Isaac Blankensmith

“My parents both went to RISD, so I had been hearing about it forever. But since I’d been thinking about going into engineering, I really had no interest in coming here myself. Then I visited – and as soon as I got to see the ID department, I realized it was exactly what I had been looking for. Being here has changed everything. It has made me really think about my own work and what it means and where I fit in.”

Student Work

Adam Smith | associate professor + department head

“RISD’s ID department has changed a lot since I was a student here in the 1980s. But it has always embraced big, basic ideas about the role of design in society – about our social responsibility as designers, about sustainability, materials research and innovation, systems thinking, public service and more recently, social entrepreneurship. There’s a lot of heart, soul and conscience behind everything we do here and how we encourage students to approach their work.”

After RISD ID alumni go on to make a real difference through design, offering innovative solutions to a wide range of needs. They start their own businesses, join cutting-edge studios and work for organizations and corporations, contributing to almost every field imaginable by creating medical devices, household products, alternative transportation vehicles, nanotech devices, new materials and much more…

Notable Alumni

Soaib Grewal | BFA '11

"More than a billion people worldwide lack access to clean drinking water," says Soaib, who teamed up with several Brown students to launch WaterWalla. Their mission? To bring clean water to India’s slums. Soaib relocated to India right after graduation to head WaterWalla’s Mumbai office and is now making great headway in tackling this chronic public-health issue.

Melissa Armstrong | BFA '07

“My studio resembles a mad scientist’s lab,” Melissa says, referring to recent experiments with making sculpture by growing it — from sugar crystals. "I have always been really interested in the intersection of art and science." Since graduation Melissa has further pursued her passion, though a residency at the Vermont Studio Center and an NEA grant to study at the Virginia Center for the Creative Arts. Now living in Brooklyn, she’s an active member the Wayfarers artist collective and balances studio work with a day job as a sculpture conservator.
