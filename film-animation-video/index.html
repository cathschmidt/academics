BFA candidate: Margaret Kearney

"I really had no idea what it would mean to relate my environmental activism with my work in textiles, so it's been exciting to develop a process for myself." Margaret Kearney’s ability to weave formal and aesthetic concerns with her environmental activism won her national recognition as a Udall Scholar, a program that recognizes college students on the basis of their commitment to careers in the environment, health care or tribal public policy. In winning the scholarship – which is typically awarded to students majoring in science or environmental policy – Kearney became the first student from an art and design school to be recognized by the Udall Foundation since the awards were first given in 1996.

Student Work

Finding a path in life takes real creativity and persistence. With a RISD education, you'll be equipped with the tools to define success on your own terms, and to be an agent of real, vital change in your field and in the world.

John Terry: Department Head

A prolific independent filmmaker, John Terry has taught at RISD, Yale, the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee and MIT, where he was instrumental in founding MIT’s film/video program. For more than two decades, he has mentored RISD’s Film / Animation / Video students. “We develop the artist as a whole, "he shares, "so that he or she can continually adapt to the changing world. We’re teaching students how to look at new ways of understanding. If we don’t, as a society we’re condemned to a hopeless repetition of what we see on TV and in the movies.”

"The most important skill I learned at RISD was the ability to create a film from start to finish. I really learned how to become a filmmaker and think about all parts of the process – storytelling, animation, sound, how to be my own producer. This gave me a big advantage once I started working in the animation industry"

Michael Dante DiMartino ‘96 FAV co-creator / executive producer, The Legend of Korra
