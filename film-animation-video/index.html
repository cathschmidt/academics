Film/Animation/Video explores the time-based art of the moving image. In the studio you will hone your creative vision, master the skills and working methods needed to produce meaningful films and prepare for a lifetime of making; in the art world, the entertainment industry and beyond.

Degree Programs

In the Studio

In the studio, you’ll produce works in a wide range of genres — documentary, experimental or narrative film, for instance — and use diverse outlets, including installation and interactive media.

BFA candidate: Kate Harkness

“For as long as I can remember, I have been creating art – painting, drawing and sculpting. Anything I could get my hands on became an art project. This love ran parallel to my passion for entertainment, mass media and performance. During my first year at RISD, the lightbulb went off when I realized I could combine the two things I love most. And from that point on, there was no doubt I’d major in FAV and go into movies and television.”

John Terry: Department Head

“We develop the artist as a whole, so that he or she can continually adapt to the changing world. We’re teaching students how to look at new ways of understanding. If we don’t, as a society we’re condemned to a hopeless repetition of what we see on TV and in the movies.”

After RISD Film/Animation/Video alumni have what it takes to stand out and make their mark as creative professionals. As entrepreneurs or through studios such as Pixar or Dreamworks and networks like Fox, HBO and PBS, they work as writers, directors, producers, animators, cinematographers, lighting specialists, sound artists and much more….

Notable Alumni

Ryan Trecartin | ‘04 BFA

Within five years of graduation, video artist Ryan Trecartin had landed a trio of major awards, racking up more than $200,000 in art prizes. And it all started with his RISD senior degree project. Now, less than a decade later, the art critic for The New Yorker has proclaimed him “the most consequential artist to have emerged since the 1980s.” »»

Gus Van Sant | ‘75 BFA

Known for his audacious, often offbeat movies, director Gus Van Sant has built his success on a magical combination of ingenious writing, perceptive casting and a healthy obsession with detail. From Good Will Hunting to Finding Forrester to Milk, he has shown great range while maintaining an independent voice, as seen most recently inPromised Land, his 2013 feature about fracking. »»
